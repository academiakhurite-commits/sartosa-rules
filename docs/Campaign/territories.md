# Explore the Territories
Each battle takes place in one of the territories of Sartosa, named on the map. At the start of the battle, the warbands arrive from a territory they control. New players starting their first game must battle at one of the Sartosa City territories, with the new player deciding which one. If both players are playing their first game, roll off to decide.  
Subsequent battles can only take place in a territory that can be reached by at least one of the warbands. This warband must be able to connect a path from any controlled territory to the territory the battle is in via the territories they have already explored. Only for this purpose Sartosa City counts as controlled territory for all warbands.  
Opponents should agree on which territory to battle over. If they cannot, they should each propose one territory which they can reach and roll off. Players can determine the location ahead of time, so any thematic terrain can be arranged.  
Players who have fought in a territory are considered to have explored that territory and can now reach its neighbouring territory. Mark the map to show which warbands have explored which territory.  
To use the water paths the warband will need boats. If a warband controls a port and has a big ship they can explore any island or port ignoring the water paths.  

## The Advantages of Territories
A warband which wins a battle in a territory gains a foothold in that territory, with the defeated warband losing its foothold if it has one. Make a note on the map or legend for all to see. While only one warband has a foothold in a territory they are also considered to control it.
Warbands who have a foothold in a territory gain the benefits and advantages of that territory as noted in the legend; some are specific to the controlling warband only (see Hard fought territory). When applying a half price bonus always round down; you still need to roll for rare items' availability.
# Point of Interest
## Abundance of Cargo
Territories which feature the Chest symbol are particularly rich in Cargo. The winner of a battle taking place in one of these districts gains 1D3 additional Cargo chest.
## Hard Fought Territory
Territories marked with the dead fish symbol are constantly fought over. Only the warband who controls those territories can gain the benefits from them. This does not affect Abundance of Cargo.
## The Ports of Tortosa
The four ports are the entrances to Tortosa, marked on the map with an anvil. In order to use a big ship you need to have a foothold in one of the ports.
